Introduction

What is wood?

Wood is essentially secondary xylem, a type of tissue that arose from secondary growth. The secondary xylem is created by an outer ring of cells (vascular cambium) and its main function is to conduct water and minerals (Simpson, 2006).

What is wood anatomy?

Why learn wood anatomy?

Wood anatomy allows scientists to classify plant species.

Classifying plant species is crucial information for various industries.

Carpentry

Different types of wood require different methods of handling. Carpenters need to know how each wood acts for quality assurance.

Forestry

All plants interact with their environment differently. Knowing these differences allows for better forest maintenance.
