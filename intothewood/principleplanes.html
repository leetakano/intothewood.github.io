Principle Planes

Overview

There are three principle planes in wood anatomy: transverse, radial, and tangential.

Hover over the cut sections of the wood cookie to learn about them.

RADIAL
RADIAL
Refers to the longitudinal plane that cuts through the center of the plant's stem.

TRANSVERSE
TRANSVERSE
Also known as the cross-section plane. Refers to the horizontal cut across the stem of a plant.

TANGENTIAL
TANGENTIAL
A longitudinal plane that doesn't cut through the center of the plant's stem.
